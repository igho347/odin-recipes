Suya Recipe

Description

Nigerian suya is a beef barbeque recipe that spice up gatherings and parties in Nigeria.

You find suya in parties and also in busy spots every night mostly prepared and sold by people from the northern part of Nigeria.

Ingredients

  • 4 yucca (cassava) roots, peeled and sliced
  • 5 medium yellow plantains, with peels on
  • Â½ cup butter, or more as needed

Steps for preparation

  1. Place cassava and unpeeled plantains into a large pot. Fill with enough water to cover and bring to a boil.
  2. Cook until cassava is soft, about 30 minutes; drain.
  3. Remove and discard plantain peels from plantains
  4. Mash cassava and plantains with butter
  5. Beat with an electric mixer until smooth, adding more butter if needed.
  6. Portion the dough into balls.
  7. Combine spice mixture and beef in a resealable plastic bag; shake until well coated. Marinate in the refrigerator, about 30 minutes
  8. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  9. Place beef, bell pepper, onion, and mushrooms in alternating order onto skewers
  10. Cook on the preheated grill, flipping once, until meat is browned and vegetables are soft, 10 to 15 minutes
